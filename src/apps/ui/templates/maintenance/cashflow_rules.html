Cashflow Forecasting Rules

Stipulate how each cashflow method forecasts and accrues expenditure. The Cash basis is always milestone payments for every project; the Accrual basis is configured per method below. The cashflow pages default to Accrual.
How it works. A project's Cashflow Method (set on the project) selects which rule applies. On the Cash basis the cashflow shows milestone payments to council for all projects. On the Accrual basis, each project follows its method's accrual source — payments (so it matches cash) or worksteps (expenditure as each workstep is forecast/completed).

Definitions

Cash (payments) — milestone Payment rows by their forecast / actual release date. Money leaving us to council.

Accrual (expenditure) — when value is delivered. Payment milestones: accrues with the payment (Capital Grants). WorkStep progressive: each workstep's share of the work value, forecast on its workstep date and released on its actual completion (Capital Works).

Workstep value = step cost % × work value (effective = actual-where-entered-else-estimated, or estimated only), split across programs by the project's approved BFA ratios.
